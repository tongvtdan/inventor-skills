## Invocation

```
/inventor-skills:invent
/inventor-skills:invent A healthcare wearable that is clinically accurate but cheap enough for home use
/inventor-skills:invent A delivery service that is both fast and low-cost
/inventor-skills:invent Smart packaging that reduces waste without increasing production cost
```

Run the full inventor pipeline — SIT divergence followed by TRIZ contradiction resolution — for: $ARGUMENTS

Execute in sequence:

**Phase 1 — SIT: Diverge**
Apply all 5 SIT thinking tools to generate 8–12 innovation concepts from the existing components and attributes of the subject. Identify the top 3 concepts by novelty and feasibility.

**Phase 2 — TRIZ: Resolve**
For each of the top 3 SIT concepts, identify the key contradiction it introduces. Apply the TRIZ Contradiction Matrix or Separation Principles. Generate 2–3 inventive resolutions per concept.

**Phase 3 — Invention Brief**
Synthesize a structured **Invention Brief** for each top concept containing:
- The SIT-generated innovation concept
- The contradiction it introduces
- The TRIZ resolution strategy
- The Ideal Final Result
- Recommended experiments or prototype steps

Refer to the TRIZ skill and SIT skill for all methodology details, principles, and output formats.

If $ARGUMENTS is empty, ask the user what they want to invent or what challenge they are facing before starting.
